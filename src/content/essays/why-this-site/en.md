Most ideas only live in chat logs and note apps. Months later even I cannot reconstruct *why* I thought something.

This site is for two plain jobs:

1. **Finish longer arguments** — not feeds, not one-line slogans, but text someone can read end to end.
2. **Keep work in one place** — projects, short notes, occasional stage or activities, visible from the home page.

## Where the form comes from

The layout follows [darioamodei.com](https://darioamodei.com): a narrow column, serif type, an expandable bio, and lists by kind of writing. I like sites that are almost only words — no card grids, no loud motion. Content carries the weight.

## v1 is intentionally sparse

There are few posts; project links may still be placeholders. The frame comes first: add a Markdown pair (English + Chinese) and it shows up on the home page. Thin and true beats padded adjectives.
