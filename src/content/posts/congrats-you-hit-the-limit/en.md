There is something quietly brilliant about AI pricing.

The same thing—usage—feels completely different in consumer and enterprise markets.

For consumers, a subscription with usage caps turns usage into a progress bar of value.

The more you use, the more it feels like you are getting your money's worth. Hitting the limit can even feel like an achievement. Then the reset starts a new cycle.

But with pay-as-you-go APIs, every call feels like a cost. The natural instinct is to reduce tokens, cache more, call less, and optimize spend.

For businesses, it flips again.

A growing API bill often means growing product usage, more customers, and more revenue. It becomes COGS—not just “money spent.”

So the same token becomes two different products:

- For consumers, it is a **subscription benefit**.
- For companies, it is a **production input**.

That is not just pricing. It is very smart psychological accounting.
